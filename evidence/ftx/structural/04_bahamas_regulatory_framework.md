# Bahamas Regulatory Framework — Securities Commission of The Bahamas (SCB)

## The DARE Act (2020) — from Fortune (November 18, 2022)

Source: Fortune, "The Bahamas pulled out all the stops to be a global crypto hub. FTX's demise could end that dream"

The Bahamas established itself as a crypto destination through two 2020 laws: the Digital Assets and Registered Exchanges (DARE) Act and the Financial and Corporate Service Providers Act. These regulations "streamlined a pathway for key digital asset services, including the issuing and trading of crypto tokens."

FTX relocated to Nassau in September 2021, attracted by regulatory clarity unavailable elsewhere. The government welcomed FTX enthusiastically — Prime Minister Philip Davis attended a groundbreaking ceremony for a proposed $60 million headquarters in April 2022 (construction never commenced).

## Regulatory Gaps

The DARE Act did NOT require:
- Segregation of customer funds from the exchange's own funds
- Regular independent audits of customer balances
- Real-time monitoring of on-chain flows

The SCB was a small regulatory body with limited staff and technical expertise for supervising complex crypto operations. It relied heavily on self-reporting by regulated entities.

## SCB Actions During the Collapse (November 2022)

- **November 10**: SCB froze FTX Digital Markets' registration
- **November 12**: SCB ordered FTX crypto transferred to government-controlled wallets
- FTX announced facilitating Bahamian withdrawals while pausing others, but "the Commission denied directing this action"
- SCB ultimately took custody of FTX deposits valued at over $3.5 billion
- Asset seizure led to jurisdictional disputes with US bankruptcy proceedings

## Post-FTX Reforms — from CoinDesk (April 26, 2023)

Source: CoinDesk, "Bahamas Seeks to Tighten Its Crypto Laws Following FTX Collapse"

The SCB announced consultation for new legislation:

**Governance Requirements**: "Operators of a digital-asset exchange must ensure the systems and controls used in its activities are adequate and appropriate for the scale and nature of its business."

**Stablecoin Framework**: "a new and comprehensive regulatory framework for stablecoins" pegged to stable assets.

**Extended Coverage**: The bill addresses crypto derivatives, advice, staking, and NFT oversight.

Christina Rolle, executive director of the Bahamas' regulator, stated the reforms could become "among the most advanced pieces of digital-asset legislation in the world."

## Political Context (from Fortune)

Opposition leader Michael Pintard raised concerns about whether politicians received FTX donations and whether adequate due diligence occurred. Prime Minister Davis asserted the country bore "no deficiencies" in regulations that could have prevented the fraud.
