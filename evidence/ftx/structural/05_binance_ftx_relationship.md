# Binance-FTX Relationship History

## The Early Partnership (2019)

Binance was an early investor in FTX, backing the exchange when it launched in 2019. CZ (Changpeng Zhao) and SBF (Sam Bankman-Fried) initially had a cooperative relationship. Binance's investment gave FTX early credibility and access to Binance's user base.

## Growing Rivalry (2020-2021)

As FTX grew rapidly, the relationship deteriorated:

- **Competition**: FTX was increasingly competing with Binance for the same users and markets
- **Regulatory lobbying**: CZ began hearing that SBF was "criticizing Binance in policy and regulatory circles in Washington" — lobbying against Binance behind the scenes while publicly maintaining cordial relations
- **Political positioning**: SBF was positioning himself as the "responsible" crypto leader who worked with regulators, implicitly contrasting himself with CZ/Binance

## The Equity Exit and FTT Position

In 2021, Binance exited its equity position in FTX. The terms of the exit were critical:

- Binance received approximately **$2.1 billion** worth of assets in exchange for its equity stake
- The payment included **~$500 million in FTT tokens** and **~$400 million in BNB** (Binance's own token) that FTX had held
- This left Binance with a massive FTT position — roughly **$2.1 billion** in FTT (combining the equity exit tokens and other holdings)

### The Strategic Asymmetry

As later analyzed by Michael Lewis in *Going Infinite*:
- CZ demanded payment heavily weighted toward FTT tokens
- This gave CZ a **strategic weapon** — a large enough FTT position to crash the token's price if sold
- Simultaneously, CZ required SBF to return BNB tokens that SBF could have used as "ammunition" against Binance
- The exit terms "trapped" SBF — Binance could threaten FTX's stability, but FTX couldn't reciprocate

## The Catalyst (Late October 2022)

The personal rivalry escalated in late October 2022:

1. SBF made a snarky public comment about whether CZ "is allowed to go to DC" to represent the crypto industry
2. CZ had been hearing reports about SBF lobbying against Binance in Washington
3. CZ stated: "I won't support people who lobby against other industry players behind their backs"

This personal friction combined with the CoinDesk Alameda balance sheet article (November 2) to trigger CZ's decision.

## CZ's November 6 Tweet

On November 6, 2022, CZ tweeted:

> "As part of Binance's exit from FTX equity last year, Binance received roughly $2.1 billion USD equivalent in cash (BUSD and FTT). Due to recent revelations that have came to light, we have decided to liquidate any remaining FTT on our books."

He added: "We will try to do so in a way that minimizes market impact. Due to market conditions and limited liquidity, we expect this will take a few months to complete."

This tweet was the match that lit the fire. The implications:
- **$2.1B in sell pressure** announced for a token with thin liquidity
- **From the most powerful player in crypto** — giving it maximum credibility
- **"A few months"** suggested sustained selling pressure, not a one-time dump
- Combined with the Alameda balance sheet leak, it confirmed the reflexive FTT risk

## Why This Is Structural

This isn't just a timeline event — it's a **structural position** that existed before the crisis:

1. **Binance held enough FTT to crash the market** — this was true since the equity exit in 2021
2. **The thin FTT order book couldn't absorb large sells** — a structural property of the token's supply distribution
3. **CZ had both motive (rivalry) and means (FTT holdings) to threaten FTX** — these conditions predated the crisis
4. **The FTT-as-collateral structure meant any large sell threatened Alameda's solvency** — mathematical certainty given the balance sheet

Given these structural facts, the answer to "What happens if Binance sells its FTT?" — a reflexive death spiral — is deterministic, not probabilistic.
