# SBF: The Media Profile

## The Congressional Witness

Sam Bankman-Fried testified before the House Financial Services Committee multiple times. He positioned himself as the "reasonable" crypto leader who actively wanted regulation — a bridge between the industry and Washington. He advocated for regulatory frameworks that would legitimize crypto, meeting regularly with regulators, senators, and White House officials.

## Magazine Covers, Profiles, TV Appearances

Forbes put him on the cover in October 2021: "The $26 Billion Man." Fortune ran a profile headlined "The Next Warren Buffett?" The New York Times profiled him in May 2022 under the headline "The Most Generous Billionaire." He appeared frequently on Bloomberg, CNBC, and other mainstream financial outlets. The consistent framing: the "adult in the room" compared to other crypto figures.

## Political Money

SBF was the second-largest individual donor to Democrats in the 2022 cycle, contributing roughly $40 million. Ryan Salame, FTX co-CEO, donated to Republican candidates. Total political spending across FTX-associated individuals: $70-100 million.

## The Effective Altruism Brand

SBF publicly committed to the EA philosophy — earning money to give it all away. He pledged to donate the majority of his wealth. He lived in a shared house in the Bahamas. He drove a Toyota Corolla despite a $26 billion net worth. The narrative this created: "He can't be stealing because he wants to give it all away."

## FTX Arena

$135 million naming rights deal with the Miami Heat (2021). "FTX Arena" seen by millions of basketball fans nightly. Companies that name NBA stadiums are supposed to be stable, established businesses.

## Celebrity Endorsements and Super Bowl Ad

Tom Brady and Gisele Bundchen — investors and brand ambassadors. Steph Curry appeared in commercials. Shaquille O'Neal served as ambassador. Kevin O'Leary was an investor and public advocate. Larry David starred in FTX's Super Bowl commercial in February 2022 — "Don't miss out on crypto" — one of the most expensive advertising slots in the world.
