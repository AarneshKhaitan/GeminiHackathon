# FTX Withdrawal Crisis — On-Chain Data Report

## Net Outflows by Day

Sunday Nov 6 (after CZ's liquidation tweet):
- Withdrawals began accelerating
- Net outflows: ~$653 million in 24 hours

Monday Nov 7:
- Withdrawal requests surged
- Estimated $1-2 billion in net outflows
- Processing delays began appearing
- Users reporting withdrawal requests stuck in "pending" for hours

Tuesday Nov 8 (before Binance announcement):
- Withdrawal processing ground to a halt
- FTX appeared to run out of liquid assets to meet withdrawal requests
- Total estimated outflows over 72 hours: approximately $6 billion
- FTX was unable to meet all withdrawal requests

## On-Chain Wallet Tracking

Blockchain watchers tracked FTX's known wallets in real time:

- FTX hot wallets showed rapid depletion of stablecoin reserves
- ETH and other crypto being moved to meet withdrawals
- Alameda wallets showed unusual activity — funds moving between Alameda and FTX addresses
- Fund movement FROM Alameda TO FTX wallets observed — suggesting FTX was pulling from Alameda to cover withdrawals

Nansen data:
- FTX.com stablecoin reserves dropped from ~$1.5B to near zero
- Significant token movements between FTX and Alameda wallets
- Outflows far exceeded any previous withdrawal spike in FTX history

## User-Reported Withdrawal Status

While SBF was tweeting "assets are fine" and "we process all withdrawals":
- Users reporting 12+ hour delays
- Some withdrawals being rejected
- On-chain data showed FTX wallet balances dropping precipitously

## Comparable Bank Run Scale

- SVB bank run: ~$42B in a day, but SVB had ~$209B in assets
- FTX reported assets: ~$16B (largely illiquid)
- $6B outflows from ~$16B exchange = roughly 37% of assets withdrawn in 3 days
- Liquid portion of FTX's assets may have been as low as $1-2B
