# Pre-Crisis Skepticism About FTX: The Minority Voices

Most coverage of FTX was positive. But some people were asking questions.

## The Alameda-FTX Problem

Some analysts questioned the relationship between FTX (exchange) and Alameda Research (trading firm). Both founded by SBF. Both based in the Bahamas. Sharing office space. "How can a trading firm get fair execution on an exchange when its founder also owns the exchange?" Alameda was widely believed to have preferential access to FTX's order flow and liquidation data.

## Business Model Questions

FTX's rapid growth was viewed with skepticism by some traditional finance analysts. Trading fee margins seemed too thin to justify the valuation. Revenue disclosures were limited and unaudited. "Where is all the money coming from?"

## Crypto Native Critics

Some crypto researchers raised concerns about FTT tokenomics and circular value. Questions about FTX's proof-of-reserves methodology. Blockchain analysts noted unusual patterns in FTX wallet activity. "Dirty Bubble Media" — a pseudonymous Substack author — published critical analysis of crypto companies including FTX-adjacent entities.

## General Crypto Skeptics

Warren Buffett: "rat poison squared." Jamie Dimon (JPMorgan) compared crypto to "pet rocks." These were general anti-crypto views, not FTX-specific, but they provided the intellectual framework for skepticism.

## Specific Red Flags

Some risk analysts noted that FTX's insurance fund claims didn't match on-chain evidence. Questions about whether FTX US and FTX.com were truly separated as claimed. Concerns about Bahamas-based regulation being insufficient for a major exchange.
