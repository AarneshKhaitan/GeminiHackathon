# FTX Positive Coverage Compilation

## Regulatory Positioning

FTX US was registered as a money services business. FTX.com was licensed in the Bahamas under the DARE Act. SBF publicly advocated for regulation, unlike many crypto leaders. FTX applied for and in some cases received licenses in multiple jurisdictions. The narrative from coverage: "FTX welcomes regulation because they have nothing to hide."

## The VC Backing

Sequoia Capital invested $214 million at a $32 billion valuation. They published a glowing profile of SBF — since deleted after the collapse — describing him as a "once-in-a-generation" talent. SoftBank was a major investor. Tiger Global made a significant investment. The Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan put in $95 million. Total VC funding exceeded $1.8 billion across multiple rounds.

"Sequoia wouldn't invest $214 million without doing due diligence." That was the line.

## Sponsorship Deals

FTX Arena (Miami Heat): $135M naming rights. Official crypto exchange of Major League Baseball. Sponsorship deal with Mercedes-AMG Petronas F1 team. These partnerships theoretically required contracts, compliance checks, and corporate governance standards.

## The Crypto Lender of Last Resort (May-June 2022)

When the Terra/LUNA crash and Three Arrows Capital collapse hit crypto in mid-2022, FTX and SBF stepped in to provide rescue financing. Extended a $400M credit line to BlockFi. SBF was compared to "J.P. Morgan during the Panic of 1907." Coverage framed it as proof of strength: "FTX is so strong it can bail out others."

## Proof of Reserves

FTX claimed to hold customer deposits 1:1. Promoted transparency initiatives. These claims were later found to be false.
