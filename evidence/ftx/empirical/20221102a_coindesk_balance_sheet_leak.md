# Divisions in Sam Bankman-Fried's Crypto Empire Blur on His Trading Titan Alameda's Balance Sheet

By Ian Allison | Edited by Nick Baker
Published Nov 2, 2022, 2:44 p.m. | Updated Aug 16, 2023, 9:56 p.m.

Sam Bankman-Fried's cryptocurrency empire officially separates into two main entities: FTX (his exchange) and Alameda Research (his trading firm). However, this division blurs significantly when examining Alameda's financial records, according to a private balance sheet CoinDesk reviewed.

As of June 30, Alameda maintained $14.6 billion in assets. The composition reveals a concerning concentration: $3.66 billion consisted of "unlocked FTT," while another $2.16 billion represented "FTT collateral." Among liabilities totaling $8 billion, $292 million involved "locked FTT."

FTT is the exchange token issued by FTX, granting holders trading fee discounts and other benefits. Approximately 197 million FTT tokens worth $5.1 billion circulated at the time, according to FTX's website.

"It's fascinating to see that the majority of the net equity in the Alameda business is actually FTX's own centrally controlled and printed-out-of-thin-air token," said Cory Klippsten, CEO of Swan Bitcoin.

Additional significant assets included $3.37 billion in "crypto held" and substantial Solana holdings: $292 million unlocked SOL, $863 million locked SOL, and $41 million in SOL collateral. Bankman-Fried had invested early in Solana. Other notable token positions included SRM, MAPS, OXY, and FIDA, plus $134 million in cash and $2 billion in equity securities.

A footnote cautioned that "locked tokens conservatively treated at 50% of fair value marked to FTX/USD order book."

FTX maintained a token buyback program consuming one-third of exchange trading commissions, continuing until half of all tokens were burned.

Alameda CEO Caroline Ellison declined comment. FTX did not respond to requests for comment.

The arrangement highlighted unusually tight connections between the two Bankman-Fried enterprises, raising questions about the trading firm's true financial foundation and independence from its sister exchange.
