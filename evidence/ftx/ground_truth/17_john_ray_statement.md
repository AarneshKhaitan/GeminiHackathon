# John Ray III First-Day Declaration — Court Filing Excerpts

Filed with the Delaware bankruptcy court, November 17, 2022. John J. Ray III, new CEO and Chief Restructuring Officer.

> "Never in my career have I seen such a complete failure of corporate controls and such a complete absence of trustworthy financial information as occurred here. From compromised systems integrity and faulty regulatory oversight abroad, to the concentration of control in the hands of a very small group of inexperienced, unsophisticated and potentially compromised individuals, this situation is unprecedented."

Ray previously oversaw the liquidation of Enron.

On financial records:

> "The FTX Group did not maintain centralized control of its cash. There were no cash management systems, and no accurate list of bank accounts or signatories."

On corporate governance:

> "One of the most pervasive failures of the FTX.com business in particular is the absence of lasting records of decision-making. Mr. Bankman-Fried often communicated by using applications that were set to auto-delete after a short period of time, and the company did not maintain books and records at its alleged principal place of business."

On employee expenses:

> "Corporate funds of the FTX Group were used to purchase homes and other personal items for employees and advisors. I understand that there does not appear to be documentation for certain of these transactions as loans, and that certain real estate was recorded in the personal name of these employees and advisors."

On accounting:

> "In the Bahamas, corporate records were not maintained in the ordinary course. As of the date of the petition, I have not been able to identify an official balance sheet for Alameda."

On software controls:

There was a "backdoor" in FTX's accounting software that "allowed Alameda Research to maintain a negative account balance — effectively a line of credit funded by customer deposits — without triggering FTX's risk management protocols."
