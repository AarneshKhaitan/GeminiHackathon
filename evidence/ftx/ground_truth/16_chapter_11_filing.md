# FTX Files Chapter 11 Bankruptcy

November 11, 2022.

FTX Trading Ltd., along with approximately 130 affiliated companies including Alameda Research, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in the District of Delaware.

Filing details:
- Sam Bankman-Fried resigned as CEO
- John J. Ray III appointed as CEO and Chief Restructuring Officer
- Filing covered FTX.com (international), FTX US, Alameda Research, and roughly 130 other entities
- Estimated liabilities: $10-50 billion
- Estimated assets: $10-50 billion (broad range reflecting uncertainty)
- Over 1 million creditors estimated

## SBF Statement

> "I'm sorry. That's the biggest thing. I f***ed up, and should have done better."

SBF posted a lengthy Twitter thread attempting to explain. Key passage:

> "FTX International currently has a total market value of assets/collateral higher than client deposits. But that's different from liquidity — as you can tell, it was not possible to convert that to USD (or other) fast enough."

He maintained that FTX US was solvent. This was later disputed. The criminal trial established that approximately $8 billion in customer funds had been transferred to Alameda and lost.
