# United States v. Samuel Bankman-Fried — DOJ / Trial Report

## Arrest and Indictment

December 12, 2022 — Sam Bankman-Fried arrested in the Bahamas at the request of the U.S. government. Indicted by federal grand jury, Southern District of New York, December 13.

Charges:
- Wire fraud (2 counts)
- Conspiracy to commit wire fraud (2 counts)
- Conspiracy to commit commodities fraud
- Conspiracy to commit securities fraud
- Conspiracy to commit money laundering
- Campaign finance violations

## Trial — October 2023

Trial ran October 3 to November 2, 2023, Manhattan federal court.

**Caroline Ellison** (Alameda CEO, cooperating witness):
- SBF directed her to use FTX customer funds to cover Alameda's losses
- Alameda had a secret backdoor exemption from FTX's liquidation engine
- SBF told her to create misleading balance sheets for lenders
- ~$8 billion in customer funds transferred to Alameda

**Gary Wang** (FTX co-founder, cooperating witness):
- Wrote the code that gave Alameda the special exemption
- Described the "allow_negative" flag that let Alameda borrow unlimited customer funds
- SBF directed the technical implementation

**Nishad Singh** (FTX engineering director, cooperating witness):
- Corroborated the other witnesses
- Described concerns he raised that were dismissed by SBF

## Verdict

November 2, 2023. Guilty on all 7 counts:

1. Wire fraud on FTX customers
2. Conspiracy to commit wire fraud on FTX customers
3. Wire fraud on Alameda Research lenders
4. Conspiracy to commit wire fraud on Alameda lenders
5. Conspiracy to commit securities fraud on FTX investors
6. Conspiracy to commit commodities fraud
7. Conspiracy to commit money laundering

## Sentencing

March 28, 2024 — 25 years in federal prison.

Judge noted SBF showed no remorse and had perjured himself during trial.
