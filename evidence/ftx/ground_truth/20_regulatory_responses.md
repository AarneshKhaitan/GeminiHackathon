# Regulatory Responses to FTX Collapse — Compiled Press Releases

## SEC — December 13, 2022

Securities and Exchange Commission charged SBF:

> "From at least May 2019 through November 2022, Bankman-Fried raised more than $1.8 billion from equity investors, including approximately $1 billion from approximately 90 U.S.-based investors. He did so by promoting FTX as a safe, responsible crypto asset trading platform. In reality, Bankman-Fried orchestrated a massive, years-long fraud, diverting billions of dollars of FTX customer funds for his own personal benefit and to help grow his crypto empire."

SEC Chair Gary Gensler:

> "We allege that Sam Bankman-Fried built a house of cards on a foundation of deception while telling investors that it was one of the safest buildings in crypto."

## CFTC

Commodity Futures Trading Commission complaint and judgment:
- $12.7 billion total judgment
- $8.7 billion in restitution for victims
- $4.0 billion in disgorgement

CFTC Commissioner Kristin Johnson:

> "FTX touted itself as 'the safest and easiest way to buy and sell crypto,' and stated FTX segregated customer assets from FTX's own assets as a general principle, when in fact customer funds were commingled and misappropriated."

## Global Regulatory Acceleration

- **EU**: MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) regulation finalized and enacted, taking effect 2024
- **US**: Multiple congressional hearings, SEC enforcement actions against other exchanges (Binance, Coinbase), proposed legislation
- **UK**: FCA stepped up crypto oversight
- **Hong Kong**: New licensing framework for crypto exchanges
- **Singapore**: MAS tightened requirements
- **Japan**: Already had strict requirements; FTX Japan customers were relatively protected due to local segregation rules

## FTX Estate Recovery

Under John Ray III's management:
- Recovered approximately $14.5-16 billion in assets
- Creditors expected to receive roughly 119% of claims (at USD value as of November 2022 filing date)
- At current crypto prices, recovery represents a much lower percentage of what the crypto would have been worth
- Recovery came from selling FTX venture investments, recovering Bahamas assets, liquidating remaining crypto
