# Investors Withdraw Millions From FTX as Binance Begins Liquidating FTT Token

By Andrew Asmakov
Decrypt, Nov 7, 2022

Crypto exchange FTX faces mounting pressure as major investors pull assets from the platform. Binance CEO Changpeng "CZ" Zhao announced he's liquidating the company's holdings of FTX's native token, FTT.

The exodus is significant. Weekly stablecoin outflows from FTX have reached $451 million, while Binance has seen net inflows exceeding $411 million during the same period, according to data from Nansen.

Jump Trading transferred approximately $40.4 million in USDC stablecoin away from FTX within 24 hours, though the firm subsequently returned $6.1 million back to the exchange. Beyond stablecoins, roughly $94 million in various cryptocurrencies departed FTX, including $73.9 million in Ethereum sent to lending firm Nexo.

## The Trigger

Reports revealed that Alameda Research — founded by FTX CEO Sam Bankman-Fried — holds approximately $5.8 billion in FTT tokens. This massive exposure prompted concerns about potential liquidity problems if FTT's value declined substantially.

CZ responded by announcing: "Due to recent revelations that have come to light, we have decided to liquidate any remaining FTT on our books." The token subsequently dropped from $25.55 to $22.39.

## The Response

Bankman-Fried dismissed the concerns as "a bunch of unfounded rumors." Alameda Research CEO Caroline Ellison countered that the reported balance sheet represented only a subset of corporate entities and claimed the firm possesses over $10 billion in assets not reflected in those figures.
