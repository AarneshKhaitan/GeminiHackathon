# Crypto Reacts to the Alameda Balance Sheet Leak: Nov 2-6

The CoinDesk article dropped on November 2. For four days, crypto debated what it meant. The reaction was split.

## The Dismissive Camp

"Alameda is a separate company. Its balance sheet says nothing about FTX customer funds."

"Trading firms often have concentrated positions. This is normal."

"Alameda has been profitable for years. They know what they're doing."

"This is from June. Things could have changed dramatically since then."

"Alameda has had months to rebalance if needed."

"FTT has held its value for years. Just because Alameda holds a lot doesn't make it worthless."

"The burn mechanism supports the token price."

"Sam has been transparent about everything. If there were a problem, he'd say so."

"He just bailed out BlockFi. FTX clearly has the resources."

## The Alarmed Camp

"40% of Alameda's assets are in a token that FTX prints. This is Luna/Terra all over again."

"If FTT drops 50%, Alameda is insolvent. There's no margin of safety."

"$134 million in cash against $7.4 billion in loans. This is a bank run waiting to happen."

"Try unwinding $3.66 billion in FTT without crashing the market. You can't."

"We saw this exact structure with LUNA. Token backs protocol backs token. It's recursive."

"Every time someone says 'the entities are separate,' they're not."

## FTT Price Action (Nov 2-5)

FTT dropped about 5-10% in the days after the article. No panic selling yet. Volume increased as traders positioned for both outcomes. BTC and ETH were roughly flat — this was FTT-specific.
