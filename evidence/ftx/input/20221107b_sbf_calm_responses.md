# SBF's Tweets During the Crisis: November 7-8, 2022

As withdrawals accelerated on November 7-8, SBF posted a series of public statements on Twitter.

## November 7 (paraphrased from archived sources):

"FTX is fine. Assets are fine."

"FTX has enough to cover all client holdings. We don't invest client assets (even in treasuries)."

"We have been processing all withdrawals, and will continue to do so."

"A competitor is trying to go after us with false rumors. FTX is fine. Assets are fine."

He also replied to individual users asking about withdrawal delays, consistently stating that everything was operational.

## November 8 (before the Binance deal announcement):

SBF continued posting as the situation deteriorated:

"We're seeing some withdrawal delays as we process a large volume of requests. This is normal during periods of high activity."

He posted a thread attempting to address the Alameda balance sheet concerns, suggesting that the CoinDesk article was based on incomplete or outdated information.

The claim "we don't invest client assets" was later proven false. Approximately $8 billion in customer funds had been transferred to Alameda. The tweets were later deleted.
