"I should have been more careful. I should have been paying more attention to what was going on. I got distracted by other things and didn't pay enough attention to the risk."

On customer funds:
He maintained that FTX International had assets worth more than client deposits in total, but acknowledged the assets were not liquid enough to meet withdrawal demands. Framed it as liquidity crisis, not solvency crisis.

"If there's one thing I could do, I would give anything to be able to do it, and I can't — it would be, this was a liquidity crisis, not a solvency crisis."

Evasive about the specific relationship between FTX customer funds and Alameda. Acknowledged "mistakes" but avoided directly admitting to commingling.
