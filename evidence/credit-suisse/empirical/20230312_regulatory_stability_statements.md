# Regulatory Stability Statements — March 2023

Multiple regulators issued reassuring statements. Raw text below.

## US Federal Reserve / FDIC / Treasury Joint Statement (March 12, 2023)

"After receiving a recommendation from the boards of the FDIC and the Federal Reserve, and consulting with the President, Secretary Yellen approved actions enabling the FDIC to complete its resolution of Silicon Valley Bank, Santa Clara, California, in a manner that fully protects all depositors. Depositors will have access to all of their money starting Monday, March 13, 2023. No losses associated with the resolution of Silicon Valley Bank will be borne by the taxpayer."

"We are also announcing a similar systemic risk exception for Signature Bank, New York, New York, which was closed today by its state chartering authority."

"The U.S. banking system remains resilient and on a solid foundation, in large part due to reforms that were made after the financial crisis that ensured better safeguards for the banking industry."

## ECB Governing Council (March 16, 2023)

The ECB proceeded with its planned 50 basis point rate hike on March 16 — a deliberate signal that it viewed the banking system as stable enough to continue monetary tightening.

ECB press conference excerpt:
President Lagarde: "The euro area banking sector is resilient, with strong capital and liquidity positions. In any case, the ECB's policy toolkit is fully equipped to provide liquidity support to the euro area financial system if needed and to preserve the smooth transmission of monetary policy."

## SNB/FINMA Joint Statement (March 15, 2023)

Published on the evening of March 15, after CS stock crashed 30%:

"Credit Suisse meets the capital and liquidity requirements imposed on systemically important banks. If necessary, the SNB will provide CS with liquidity."

Full text was brief — just two sentences. The brevity itself is notable. This is the entirety of the formal regulatory reassurance for a G-SIB whose stock just crashed 30%.

## Bank of England (March 13-14, 2023)

"The UK banking system is well capitalised and funded, and remains safe and sound."

The BoE also facilitated the sale of SVB UK to HSBC for £1, protecting all UK depositors.

## ECB Banking Supervision (Andrea Enria, March 2023)

"I want to emphasize that the European banking sector has no meaningful exposure to SVB. European banks are fundamentally different from the US regional banks that have been in the headlines."

## Note on the SNB/FINMA statement specifically:

The SNB/FINMA statement is the most important for the CS case. On its face it's reassuring: the regulators are vouching for CS. But consider:

1. The SNB doesn't issue joint statements about UBS meeting its requirements. Healthy banks don't need public regulatory endorsements.

2. "If necessary, the SNB will provide CS with liquidity" — if CS truly meets requirements, why preemptively offer emergency facilities?

3. The next day (March 16), CS drew CHF 50 billion from the SNB. That's the largest emergency draw in Swiss banking history.

4. Historical comparison: SEC Chairman Chris Cox said Bear Stearns had "a strong capital position" on March 11, 2008. Bear was acquired by JPMorgan on March 16, 2008.
