# CREDIT SUISSE GROUP AG FOURTH QUARTER AND FULL YEAR 2022 RESULTS

Source: SEC Filing, Exhibit 99.1, February 9, 2023

ZURICH, February 9, 2023 – Credit Suisse today reported its 4Q22 and full year 2022 results.

Selected financial highlights (reported):

Fourth quarter 2022:
- Net revenues: CHF 3,057 million
- Provision for credit losses: CHF 78 million
- Total operating expenses: CHF 4,235 million
- Pre-tax loss: CHF (1,391) million
- Net loss attributable to shareholders: CHF (1,393) million

Full year 2022:
- Net revenues: CHF 14,921 million (down from CHF 22,696 million in 2021)
- Net loss attributable to shareholders: CHF (7,293) million
- This compared to a net loss of CHF (1,572) million in 2021

Capital:
- CET1 ratio: 14.1% (vs 14.4% at end of 2021)
- CET1 capital: CHF 36.0 billion
- Tier 1 leverage ratio: 7.7%
- Total capital ratio: approximately 20.0%

THE CRITICAL DISCLOSURE — DEPOSIT OUTFLOWS:

"Net new asset outflows for the Group were CHF (110.5) billion in 4Q22"

This number is buried in the release but it's the single most important data point. CHF 110.5 billion in net outflows in a single quarter. That's roughly a quarter of the deposit base leaving in 90 days.

Context from the earnings call (Motley Fool transcript):

CEO Ulrich Koerner on the earnings call:
"We saw substantial deposit and net asset outflows across our wealth management-related divisions in October, primarily driven by adverse social media coverage and resulting media attention. Outflows moderated as we moved into November and December, and we have started to see net inflows return in January 2023."

"Let me also reiterate that we are determined to execute on our comprehensive transformation."

"The actions we are taking will deliver a simpler, more focused and more stable bank for our clients, shareholders and employees."

CFO Dixit Joshi on capital:
"Our CET1 ratio was 14.1% at year-end and compares favorably to our regulatory requirements and to peers."

Analyst questions included:
- Whether deposit outflows had truly stabilized
- Whether the capital raise would be sufficient
- Whether the restructuring timeline was realistic
- Whether CS could retain key talent during the transformation

Some analysts on the call noted the "disconnect" between the stable capital ratios and the massive deposit flight — essentially asking whether CS was burning through its liquid assets to maintain the appearance of stability.

Other items from the filing:
- Litigation provisions: CHF 690 million for 4Q22
- Restructuring charges: CHF 393 million
- Investment banking revenue: down significantly year over year
- Wealth management: net asset outflows across all regions
- Swiss bank: relatively stable, but not immune

Revenue comparison:
2021: CHF 22,696 million
2022: CHF 14,921 million
That's a 34% revenue decline. The business was shrinking fast.
