# "European banks are not US regional banks" — analyst and regulatory consensus, March 2023

Compiled from multiple sources: Reuters, Bloomberg, ECB, analyst notes — March 10-16, 2023

In the days following Silicon Valley Bank's failure, a chorus of analysts, strategists, and regulators insisted that European banks were fundamentally different from US regional banks and that contagion fears were overblown.

ECB Vice-President Luis de Guindos, March 13: "European banks are in a completely different situation from SVB. They have strong capital and liquidity positions."

Andrea Enria, Chair of ECB Banking Supervision: "I want to emphasize that the European banking sector has no meaningful exposure to SVB. European banks are fundamentally different from the US regional banks that have been in the headlines."

The ECB proceeded with its planned 50 basis point rate hike on March 16, sending a deliberate signal that it viewed the banking system as stable enough to continue monetary tightening.

Stuart Graham, Autonomous Research analyst: "The sell-off in European banks is an overreaction. European banks have been stress-tested repeatedly, hold far more capital than US regionals, and don't have the same interest rate exposure."

JPMorgan European banks research team, March 13: "We see very limited read-across from SVB to European banks. The business models, regulatory frameworks, and interest rate exposures are fundamentally different."

Key structural differences cited:
- European banks subject to full Basel III implementation; US regional banks exempted from many requirements under 2018 Economic Growth Act
- European banks undergo annual EBA/ECB stress tests
- European banks hold higher Liquidity Coverage Ratios (LCR)
- European bank deposit bases are more diversified (not concentrated in one industry)
- European banks have access to ECB lending facilities

Bank of England statement, March 14: "UK banks are well capitalised and funded. The UK banking system remains safe and sound."

Multiple analysts specifically addressed Credit Suisse:

"CS has its own well-documented issues, but they have absolutely nothing to do with SVB. CS's problems are governance and strategy, not interest rate risk. Conflating them is intellectually lazy."

"European banks are not SVB. Credit Suisse is not SVB. The comparison is misleading and is causing unwarranted panic."

"The sell-off in European bank stocks is a buying opportunity. These banks are trading below book value despite strong fundamentals."

The Euro STOXX Banks index (SX7E) fell approximately 16% during March 2023 but subsequently recovered. No European bank other than Credit Suisse required intervention.
