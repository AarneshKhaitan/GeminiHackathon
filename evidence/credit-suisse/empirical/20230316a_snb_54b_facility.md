# Credit Suisse to borrow up to CHF50 billion from Swiss National Bank

SWI swissinfo.ch
Published: March 16, 2023 - 17:54

Switzerland's second largest bank announced plans to borrow up to CHF50 billion ($54 billion) from the Swiss central bank to strengthen its liquidity position following market turbulence triggered by bank failures in the United States.

"This additional liquidity would support Credit Suisse's core businesses and clients as Credit Suisse takes the necessary steps to create a simpler and more focused bank," the institution stated in a Wednesday evening announcement.

The bank also committed to accessing a short-term liquidity facility and repurchasing approximately CHF3 billion in debt.

Market reaction proved initially positive. Credit Suisse shares surged as much as 32% when trading opened Thursday, though gains moderated during the session, closing up 19% after the previous day's devastating 25% collapse.

The Swiss National Bank (SNB) and Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) released a joint statement Wednesday evening confirming readiness to provide emergency liquidity support if needed. Their statement emphasized that "problems of certain banks in the USA do not pose a direct risk of contagion for the Swiss financial markets."

This intervention demonstrated authorities' commitment to preventing collapse of a systemically important institution. The move aimed to reassure depositors and prevent potential bank runs.

Credit Suisse's largest shareholder, Saudi National Bank, had declined further capital injections. Major investor Harris Associates simultaneously announced its exit from the bank.

FINMA confirmed Credit Suisse maintained adequate capital and liquidity reserves for systemically important banks, suggesting regulatory confidence in the institution's stability despite recent turmoil.

From Banking Dive, March 16, 2023:

"Credit Suisse borrows $54B from Swiss central bank"

Credit Suisse said Thursday it would draw up to 50 billion Swiss francs ($54 billion) from the Swiss National Bank under a covered loan facility and a short-term liquidity facility, as the troubled lender seeks to ease investor fears and prevent a liquidity crisis.

The move came after the bank's shares plummeted 24% on Wednesday following comments by the chairman of its largest investor, Saudi National Bank, saying his institution would "absolutely not" add to its roughly 10% stake.

The Swiss National Bank said it was "providing liquidity" to Credit Suisse in a brief statement Thursday.

Despite the lifeline, Credit Suisse shares fell sharply again Friday — dropping another 8% — suggesting the emergency borrowing had not fully calmed nerves.
