# Saudi National Bank Chairman Interview — Bloomberg TV, March 15, 2023

Interviewee: Ammar Al Khudairy, Chairman, Saudi National Bank
Network: Bloomberg Television
Date: March 15, 2023

## Transcript of key exchange:

BLOOMBERG: Would Saudi National Bank be open to increasing your stake in Credit Suisse, if they were to have another call for fresh money, for instance?

AL KHUDAIRY: The answer is absolutely not, for many reasons outside the simplest reason, which is regulatory and statutory. We now own 9.9% of the bank. If we go above 10%, all kinds of new rules kick in, whether be it by our regulator, or the European regulator, or the Swiss regulator. So that is not something that we would consider.

He continued in the interview expressing confidence in Credit Suisse's management and restructuring plan. He also discussed broader topics including the Fed's rate path, SVB, and the Saudi economy.

When asked if he was concerned about his investment in CS, he said something to the effect of: "We are comfortable with our investment. Credit Suisse has a strong franchise and a solid restructuring plan."

## What the market did:

Credit Suisse stock on SIX Swiss Exchange (CSGN.SW):
- Trading around CHF 2.20 before the interview
- Dropped to as low as CHF 1.55 during the afternoon session
- Closed around CHF 1.70
- Intraday decline: approximately 30% at the worst point
- Trading volume exploded — over 500 million shares changed hands (normal is ~30M)

CS 5-year CDS: jumped from approximately 500 basis points to over 800 basis points during the session, briefly touching 1,000bp.

European bank index (SX7E): fell 3-5% in sympathy.

## From CNBC reporting (March 16, 2023):

"Saudi National Bank says panic over Credit Suisse is 'unwarranted'"

The day AFTER the interview, Al Khudairy tried to calm things:
"The panic in the market is a little bit unwarranted. We've invested at a price that was attractive for us. We're comfortable with our position."

He also said he maintained regular contact with CS leadership and was "satisfied with the turnaround plan."

## From CNN Business (March 15, 2023):

"Credit Suisse borrows more than $50 billion from Swiss National Bank after shares crash 30%"

"Shares of Credit Suisse sank over 30% after Saudi National Bank chairman Ammar Al Khudairy told Bloomberg Television that its biggest shareholder would not provide more money to the Swiss lender, citing regulatory constraints."

"The plunge deepened a week of turmoil for European banks, which have fallen sharply since the sudden collapse of SVB Financial."

## Aftermath:

Al Khudairy resigned as Saudi National Bank chairman on March 27, 2023, citing "personal reasons." He had been chairman since the bank's formation through the merger of National Commercial Bank and Samba Financial Group in 2021.

Saudi National Bank lost over $1 billion on its Credit Suisse investment (invested CHF 1.4B, received approximately CHF 280M worth of UBS shares at the merger price of CHF 0.76).
