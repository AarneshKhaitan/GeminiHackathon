# Credit Suisse stock and CDS spreads — March 10-14, 2023

Market data compiled from Yahoo Finance, Reuters, and Bloomberg reporting

## Credit Suisse (CSGN.SW) — SIX Swiss Exchange

Mar 8: CHF 2.82 (close)
Mar 9: CHF 2.70 (-4.3%) — SVB capital raise announcement spooks markets
Mar 10: CHF 2.52 (-6.7%) — SVB closed by regulators; FDIC appointed receiver
Mar 13: CHF 2.39 (-5.2%) — Monday after Signature Bank closure, global banking selloff
Mar 14: CHF 2.35 (-1.7%) — continued selling but pace slowing

CS was already trading near all-time lows before SVB. Shares had declined from CHF 8+ in early 2022 to under CHF 3 by March 2023.

## CDS spreads (5-year senior)

Credit Default Swap spreads — the annual cost to insure $10 million of CS debt against default:

Early March: ~280-320 basis points
Mar 10: ~380bp
Mar 13: ~420bp
Mar 14: ~450bp

For context, most large European banks were trading at 50-100bp CDS. CS was already 3-4x wider than peers BEFORE the acute crisis.

Deutsche Bank: ~100-120bp
UBS: ~50-70bp
BNP Paribas: ~45-60bp
Barclays: ~60-80bp
Credit Suisse: ~280-450bp

The divergence was already extreme. CS was being priced as a fundamentally different credit risk than its peers. The SVB shock widened everyone, but CS widened proportionally more.

Reuters, March 13: "Credit Suisse credit default swaps jumped to their highest level since the 2008 financial crisis on Monday, as investors grew increasingly worried about the health of the Swiss bank following the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank."

Bloomberg, March 14: "Credit Suisse's five-year credit default swaps climbed to around 450 basis points, signaling deep market anxiety about the bank's future despite management assertions that the restructuring remains on track."

Volume in CS stock traded was approximately 2-3x normal during this period, with heavy selling from institutional investors reducing exposure.
