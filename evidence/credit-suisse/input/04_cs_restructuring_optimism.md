# Credit Suisse shares are a 'steal,' say new Saudi backers after taking 9.9% stake

CNBC, October 31, 2022

Saudi National Bank, the kingdom's largest lender, has acquired a 9.9% stake in Credit Suisse as part of the Swiss bank's 4 billion Swiss franc ($4.1 billion) capital raise, making it the bank's largest shareholder.

Saudi National Bank CEO Ammar Al Khudairy told CNBC that Credit Suisse shares were "a steal" at the investment price.

"If you look at the franchise value — one of the top 20 investment banks, one of the top 10 wealth managers — actually it's at a discount," Al Khudairy said. "We believe in Credit Suisse's turnaround story."

The Saudi investment was part of a broader restructuring plan announced by Credit Suisse in late October 2022, which included:

- Raising CHF 4 billion in new capital from investors
- Splitting the investment bank, with the advisory business to be rebranded as "First Boston"
- Cutting approximately 9,000 jobs by 2025
- Exiting unprofitable businesses and reducing risk
- Focusing on wealth management and the Swiss domestic bank

Several analysts expressed cautious optimism:

"The capital raise gives CS the runway to execute the restructuring. If they can stabilize deposits and rebuild the investment bank franchise, there's significant upside from current levels."

"The Saudi investment is a vote of confidence from a sophisticated sovereign-adjacent investor. They wouldn't put $1.5 billion in if they didn't believe in the turnaround."

Credit Suisse CEO Ulrich Koerner, who took over in July 2022, said: "We are determined to execute on our comprehensive transformation. This capital raise gives us the resources to do so."

The capital raise was completed in November 2022, raising the full CHF 4 billion despite market skepticism.

However, the Q4 2022 earnings report, released February 9, 2023, revealed CHF 110.5 billion in deposit outflows during the quarter, casting doubt on whether the restructuring plan could be executed quickly enough to stem the bleeding.
