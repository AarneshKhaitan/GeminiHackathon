# Analyst and Commentator Defenses of Credit Suisse During Crisis Week

Throughout the week of March 13-17, 2023, a number of analysts and market commentators pushed back on the panic surrounding Credit Suisse, arguing the bank's situation was fundamentally different from the US bank failures that had spooked markets.

On the structural comparison to SVB, defenders were emphatic: "Credit Suisse is a globally systemically important bank with a 167-year history. Comparing it to a US regional bank focused on tech startups is absurd." Others pointed out that "CS's problems are governance and strategy, not interest rate risk or asset-liability mismatch. This is a completely different situation from SVB." Credit Suisse's CET1 capital ratio of 14.1% was cited repeatedly as far above regulatory minimums.

On the regulatory side, commentators noted that "FINMA would never let a G-SIB fail. The too-big-to-fail framework exists precisely for this situation." The Swiss government was seen as having every incentive to protect one of only two Swiss G-SIBs. And unlike SVB, CS had access to central bank liquidity facilities — "That's the whole point of being a regulated bank."

There were valuation arguments too. CS was trading below tangible book value. "Even in a worst-case scenario, the franchise has value." Some argued the wealth management business alone was worth more than the current market cap, and that "at these prices, you're getting the entire investment bank for free."

On March 16, the day after his "absolutely not" comments triggered the crash, Saudi National Bank chairman Ammar Al Khudairy attempted to walk back the damage. He told reporters:

> "The panic is unwarranted... the bank is being unfairly targeted."

Sources: CNBC, "Saudi National Bank says panic over Credit Suisse is 'unwarranted'" (March 16, 2023); various analyst notes and media commentary from March 13-17, 2023.
