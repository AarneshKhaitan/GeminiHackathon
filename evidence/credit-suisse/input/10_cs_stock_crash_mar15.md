# Credit Suisse Shares Crash 30% in Historic Rout

Credit Suisse shares suffered their worst day in history on March 15, 2023, plunging as much as 30% intraday after the chairman of the bank's largest shareholder, Saudi National Bank, told Bloomberg TV he would "absolutely not" provide additional capital to the struggling Swiss lender.

The stock opened around CHF 2.20 on the Swiss Exchange and cratered to an intraday low of approximately CHF 1.55 during the afternoon session following the Saudi chairman's remarks. A partial recovery into the close brought shares back to around CHF 1.70, still representing a close-to-close decline of roughly 24%.

The selloff rippled across Credit Suisse's broader capital structure. Credit default swap spreads on the bank exploded above 800 basis points, up from roughly 350bp just days earlier. CS AT1 bond prices crashed as markets priced in rising write-down risk. European bank stocks fell broadly, with the Euro STOXX Banks index (SX7E) dropping 3-5%. US bank stocks also came under pressure. The Swiss franc saw modest safe-haven inflows.

The scale of the single-day crash placed Credit Suisse in a grim historical category:

| Bank | Date | Intraday Drop | Outcome |
|------|------|---------------|---------|
| Bear Stearns | Mar 14, 2008 | -47% | Acquired by JPMorgan (Mar 16) |
| Lehman Brothers | Sep 9, 2008 | -45% | Bankruptcy (Sep 15) |
| Credit Suisse | Mar 15, 2023 | -30% | Acquired by UBS (Mar 19) |
| SVB | Mar 9, 2023 | -60% | FDIC receiver (Mar 10) |

Bear Stearns was acquired two days after its crash. Lehman filed for bankruptcy six days after its worst session. SVB was seized by the FDIC the next morning. Credit Suisse was acquired by UBS four days later.
