# Credit Suisse helped Archegos take 'potentially catastrophic' risks before losing billions when it collapsed

Source: CNN Business, July 29, 2021 | Axios, July 30, 2021

Credit Suisse published the results of an independent investigation into its $5.5 billion loss related to the collapse of Archegos Capital Management, the family office of Bill Hwang.

The 165-page report prepared by law firm Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison paints a devastating picture of risk management failures.

Key excerpts from the Paul Weiss report:

"The Archegos-related losses sustained by CS are the result of a fundamental failure of management and controls in CS's Investment Bank and, specifically, in its Prime Services business."

"The business was focused on maximizing short-term profits and failed to rein in, and, indeed enabled Archegos's voracious risk-taking."

"There was a persistent failure to escalate and remediate conspicuous risks."

"CS permitted Archegos to assume positions of such size and risk that CS's potential exposure to Archegos was far in excess of the bounds of prudent risk management."

Some numbers from the report:

- Archegos had a net-long bias of more than $7 billion at CS, compared to the next-largest long-biased client at $1.5 billion
- Archegos held a single issuer concentration of $3.3 billion, representing more than 8% of the outstanding free float
- Multiple risk managers flagged concerns. They were ignored or overridden.
- The report describes a "lackadaisical attitude toward risk" in the prime services business
- CS lost approximately $5.5 billion — the largest single loss by any prime broker from the Archegos default
- By comparison, Nomura lost ~$2.9B. Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley exited quickly with minimal losses.

Consequences:
- Several senior executives departed or were fired
- $70 million in executive bonuses clawed back
- CS's prime brokerage reputation was severely damaged
- Board-level governance reforms initiated (later derailed by further scandals)

This was the same month Greensill Capital also collapsed, exposing CS to $10B in frozen supply chain finance funds. Two multi-billion-dollar failures in a single month.

Note: Bill Hwang was later convicted of fraud and market manipulation in connection with Archegos. But CS's losses were fundamentally due to CS's own risk management failures, not Hwang's fraud — other prime brokers with the same client exited with far smaller losses because they had better controls.
