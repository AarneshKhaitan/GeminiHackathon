# BREAKING: UBS-Credit Suisse Merger Negotiated Over Weekend — Live Updates

**Wire service reporting, March 18-19, 2023**

---

**FRIDAY MARCH 17 — CLOSE OF BUSINESS**

Credit Suisse shares closed at approximately CHF 1.86, down roughly 40% over the past week. Credit default swaps had blown past 800 basis points. The bank had already tapped CHF 50 billion from the Swiss National Bank under the emergency liquidity facility announced Thursday. AT1 bonds were trading at 15-25 cents on the dollar.

Multiple sources familiar with the situation told reporters the bank would not survive another week of open trading.

---

**SATURDAY MARCH 18 — BERN**

Representatives from the Swiss government, FINMA, the Swiss National Bank, UBS, and Credit Suisse gathered in Bern for emergency talks. According to people briefed on the discussions, UBS was the only realistic acquirer — no other institution had both the balance sheet capacity and Swiss domestic presence to absorb the 167-year-old bank.

UBS put an initial offer on the table: CHF 0.25 per share, roughly $1 billion total for Credit Suisse.

The Credit Suisse board rejected the offer as too low.

Parallel working groups were set up to negotiate several interlinked issues simultaneously: the treatment of AT1 capital instruments, what government guarantees UBS would require, additional Swiss National Bank liquidity facilities for the combined entity, and — critically — a legal mechanism to bypass the normal shareholder approval requirement for a merger of this scale.

Talks continued through Saturday night.

---

**SUNDAY MARCH 19 — MORNING**

Negotiators returned to work early Sunday. The deadline was immovable: Asian markets opening Sunday evening European time. No deal by then meant FINMA would likely have to put Credit Suisse into formal resolution — a far more disorderly outcome with unpredictable contagion effects.

Derivative contracts were set to reset Monday. Counterparty risk was accumulating by the hour.

---

**SUNDAY MARCH 19 — AFTERNOON**

A deal crystallized at CHF 0.76 per share, approximately CHF 3 billion ($3.25 billion total) in an all-stock transaction. Against Credit Suisse's Friday closing price of CHF 1.86, that represented a 59% discount. Against the 52-week high of roughly CHF 7.20, it was an 89% haircut.

The Swiss Federal Council adopted emergency amendments to the existing ordinance:

- **Article 5a** authorized FINMA to order the write-down of Additional Tier 1 capital instruments for systemically important banks receiving government support.
- **Article 10a** removed the legal requirement for shareholder approval of the transaction.

---

**SUNDAY MARCH 19 — EVENING**

FINMA ordered the complete write-down of all Credit Suisse AT1 instruments — 13 publicly issued bonds worth approximately CHF 16 billion — to zero.

The Swiss National Bank authorized up to CHF 200 billion in liquidity support for the merged entity. The Swiss government committed CHF 9 billion in loss guarantees to UBS.

The deal was announced Sunday evening, ahead of Asian market opens. No shareholder vote would be held at either company.

From start to finish, the transaction was negotiated in approximately 36 hours.
