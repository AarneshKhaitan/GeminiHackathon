# Credit Suisse Bondholders Prepare Legal Action Over AT1 Wipeout

Reuters / compiled wire reports, March 19-22, 2023

---

CHF 16 billion in Credit Suisse Additional Tier 1 bonds were written to zero on Sunday night. Equity holders received CHF 0.76 per share — roughly $3.25 billion total. The bondholders got nothing.

**Quinn Emanuel Urquhart & Sullivan**, the litigation firm, released a statement: "This is an unprecedented destruction of value that violates the basic principles of creditor hierarchy."

The firm said it was organizing bondholder groups across multiple jurisdictions.

**PIMCO and Invesco**, among the world's largest fixed-income asset managers, began coordinating with other institutional holders within days. These were not retail investors — they managed trillions in combined assets.

From bondholder calls and investor communications in the days following:

"In 300 years of modern banking, creditors have always been senior to equity in insolvency. Switzerland just rewrote the rules overnight."

"If AT1 bonds can be zeroed while equity survives, what's the point of the creditor hierarchy?"

"This has destroyed the entire premise of the AT1 market."

Holders included pension funds, insurance companies, hedge funds, and asset managers spanning Asia, the Middle East, Europe, and the Americas. Some AT1 bonds were held by wealthy individuals through private banking portfolios — including Credit Suisse's own private banking clients.

---

## Legal Challenges Filed

Multiple bondholder groups filed appeals with the Swiss Federal Administrative Court challenging FINMA's write-down order.

In **October 2025**, the court ruled the write-down was **unlawful**:

- FINMA's order lacked legal basis in the bond documentation.
- Article 5a of the Emergency Ordinance was unconstitutional.
- The write-down violated the constitutional guarantee of ownership.

FINMA appealed to the Swiss Federal Supreme Court. The case remains pending.

Some bondholders also explored litigation in other jurisdictions where AT1 bonds were distributed — US courts for bonds sold under New York law, EU courts for bonds marketed to European investors.

---

## ECB and BoE Counter-Statements

Both the European Central Bank and Bank of England issued statements within hours clarifying that in EU and UK bank resolution, normal creditor hierarchy applies:

> "Common equity instruments are the first ones to absorb losses, and only after their full use would AT1 be required to be written down."

The Bank of England's Prudential Regulation Authority separately confirmed that UK resolution would follow the standard creditor hierarchy.

---

## Monday March 20 — AT1 Market

Global AT1 bonds — approximately $275 billion outstanding — sold off 5-15% on the first trading day after the announcement. Investors demanded higher yields across the asset class. Some issuers faced difficulty refinancing AT1 instruments in the weeks that followed.
