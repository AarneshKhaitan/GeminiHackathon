# Swiss Federal Council — Emergency Ordinance on Additional Liquidity Assistance and Federal Default Guarantees for Systemically Important Banks

## Media Release — Bern, March 19, 2023

The Federal Council has adopted amendments to the Emergency Ordinance on Additional Liquidity Assistance Loans and the Granting of Federal Default Guarantees for Systemically Important Banks.

### Initial Ordinance — March 16, 2023

The ordinance authorized additional Swiss National Bank liquidity facilities beyond standard programs for systemically important banks and provided federal government guarantees for emergency lending.

### Amendments — March 19, 2023

Two new articles were added:

**Article 5a — Write-Down of Additional Tier 1 Capital Instruments**

FINMA is authorized to order systemically important banks receiving government support under this ordinance to write down their Additional Tier 1 capital instruments to zero.

**Article 10a — Waiver of Shareholder Approval**

The legal requirement for a general meeting of shareholders to approve transactions involving systemically important banks under this ordinance is removed. This applies to both the acquiring and acquired institution.

---

## Timeline

- **Saturday, March 18**: Negotiations between UBS, Credit Suisse, FINMA, Swiss National Bank, and Federal Council representatives begin in Bern.
- **Sunday, March 19, morning**: Articles 5a and 10a drafted by government lawyers.
- **Sunday, March 19, afternoon**: Federal Council adopts amendments to the emergency ordinance.
- **Sunday, March 19, evening**: FINMA uses Article 5a to order complete write-down of Credit Suisse AT1 instruments (approximately CHF 16 billion).
- **Sunday, March 19, night**: UBS-Credit Suisse merger announced. No shareholder vote at either company.

From drafting of the new articles to execution of the AT1 write-down: approximately 12 hours.

The Federal Council invoked Article 185 of the Swiss Constitution, which permits the Federal Council to enact emergency ordinances to address urgent threats.

---

## Swiss Federal Administrative Court — October 2025

In ruling B-2334/2023, the Swiss Federal Administrative Court found Article 5a **unconstitutional**:

- The article violated constitutional requirements for emergency ordinances.
- It improperly delegated expropriation authority. The court held that writing down AT1 instruments to zero constituted de facto expropriation.
- It violated the constitutional guarantee of ownership.

FINMA appealed the ruling. The case is pending before the Swiss Federal Supreme Court.
