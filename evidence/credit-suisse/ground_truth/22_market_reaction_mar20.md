# Markets React to UBS-Credit Suisse Deal — Monday March 20, 2023

Wire service market reporting

---

## UBS

UBS shares opened down approximately 8% at CHF 15.20, off from Friday's close near CHF 16.50. The stock partially recovered through the session. Traders cited integration risk, potential hidden losses buried in Credit Suisse's books, possible liabilities from AT1 bondholder litigation, and the sheer complexity of absorbing a troubled global systemically important bank.

## Credit Suisse

Credit Suisse opened near the offer price of CHF 0.76 and traded in a tight range as merger arbitrage desks took over. Some shares changed hands slightly above the offer, reflecting market assessment of deal certainty.

## European Banks

The Euro STOXX Banks index posted a modest decline. The sector was caught between two forces — relief that the Credit Suisse contagion risk appeared contained through a forced merger rather than a disorderly failure, and fear over the AT1 write-down precedent.

Deutsche Bank credit default swaps widened as the market briefly fixated on the next-weakest link in European banking. French, Spanish, and Italian bank stocks held relatively stable.

## AT1 Bonds

Global AT1 bonds dropped 5-15% across the board. The $275 billion asset class repriced as investors digested the Credit Suisse write-down. AT1 spreads widened significantly. The ECB, SRB, EBA, and Bank of England clarification statements helped limit the damage but did not fully reverse it. Spreads remained elevated for months.

## Broader Markets

The S&P 500 was roughly flat on the day — Wall Street's read was that the Swiss deal contained the contagion rather than spreading it.

Gold ticked up modestly on safe-haven buying.

U.S. Treasuries rallied as investors moved into flight-to-quality trades.

The Swiss franc depreciated slightly.
