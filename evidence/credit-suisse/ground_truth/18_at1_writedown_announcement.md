# FINMA Orders Complete Write-Down of Credit Suisse AT1 Capital Instruments

## FINMA Press Release — Bern, March 19, 2023

The Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority FINMA has ordered Credit Suisse Group AG to write down its Additional Tier 1 capital instruments to zero. The write-down applies to all 13 publicly issued AT1 bonds.

Total notional value eliminated: approximately CHF 16 billion ($17.3 billion).

FINMA determined that a Viability Event had occurred. The AT1 bond prospectuses defined a Viability Event as including receipt of "irrevocable commitment of extraordinary support from the public sector." The emergency government support package — comprising CHF 200 billion in SNB liquidity backstops and CHF 9 billion in federal loss guarantees — constituted such support.

Additionally, the Federal Council enacted Article 5a of the Emergency Ordinance on March 19, which explicitly authorized FINMA to order the write-down of AT1 capital instruments for systemically important banks receiving government support.

Credit Suisse equity holders will receive CHF 0.76 per share in UBS stock under the merger agreement, representing approximately $3.25 billion in total value.

## REUTERS — March 20, 2023

**Credit Suisse AT1 bondholders wiped out as equity holders receive $3.25 billion**

ZURICH (Reuters) — Holders of CHF 16 billion in Credit Suisse Additional Tier 1 bonds were left with nothing on Sunday after Swiss regulators ordered the instruments written to zero as part of the emergency UBS takeover, even as equity holders walked away with CHF 0.76 per share.

The decision stunned credit markets. In a standard bank resolution or insolvency, equity is wiped out before bondholders take losses. AT1 bonds, while designed to absorb losses, sit above common equity in the capital structure.

"This violates the fundamental principle of creditor hierarchy," one bondholder told Reuters.

"AT1 bonds were sold as senior to equity. That contract has been violated," said another investor who held CS AT1 instruments across multiple funds.

"No one will ever buy a Swiss AT1 bond again," a London-based credit portfolio manager said.

Quinn Emanuel Urquhart & Sullivan, the law firm, confirmed it was organizing bondholder groups to pursue legal action against FINMA's order. The firm called it "an unprecedented destruction of value."

PIMCO, Invesco, and other major institutional asset managers began organizing within days of the announcement.

Multiple bondholder groups subsequently filed appeals with the Swiss Federal Administrative Court.

In **October 2025**, the court ruled the write-down was **unlawful**, finding that FINMA's order lacked proper legal basis and that Article 5a of the Emergency Ordinance was unconstitutional. The court held the write-down violated the constitutional guarantee of ownership. FINMA appealed the ruling to the Swiss Federal Supreme Court. The case remains pending.

Within hours of the Sunday night announcement, both the European Central Bank and the Bank of England issued statements clarifying that in their jurisdictions, AT1 bonds would follow normal creditor hierarchy — equity would be wiped out before AT1.

The global AT1 market — approximately $275 billion outstanding — repriced sharply downward on Monday March 20.
