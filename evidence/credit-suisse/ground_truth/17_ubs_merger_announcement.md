# FINMA PRESS RELEASE — March 19, 2023

"FINMA approves merger of UBS and Credit Suisse"

FINMA has approved the takeover of Credit Suisse by UBS, based on the bank's merger law and the Emergency Ordinance of the Federal Council of 16 March 2023 (as amended on 19 March 2023).

Key terms of the transaction:

Exchange ratio: 1 Credit Suisse share = 0.0228 UBS shares
Implied price: CHF 0.76 per Credit Suisse share
Total consideration: approximately CHF 3 billion (~USD 3.25 billion)
Form: all-stock merger

Government support:
- SNB: liquidity assistance of up to CHF 100 billion (later expanded to CHF 200 billion)
- Swiss Confederation: loss protection guarantee of up to CHF 9 billion for UBS
- FINMA: ordered complete write-down of Credit Suisse AT1 capital instruments (CHF ~16 billion)

No shareholder approval required — Article 10a of the Emergency Ordinance waived the requirement for general meetings.

---

From Reuters / CNN / various, March 19-20:

UBS CEO Ralph Hamers: "This acquisition is attractive for UBS shareholders but, let us be clear, as far as Credit Suisse is concerned, this is an emergency rescue."

Swiss Finance Minister Karin Keller-Sutter: "A bankruptcy of Credit Suisse would have had incalculable consequences for the country and the international financial system. The Federal Council regrets that these measures were necessary. They are, however, the right thing to do."

Credit Suisse 52-week high: approximately CHF 7.20

Credit Suisse tangible book value per share: approximately CHF 10+
Discount to tangible book: over 90%

Credit Suisse market cap at deal price: approximately CHF 3 billion

A 167-year-old Swiss institution, once among the five most prestigious banks in the world, sold in a weekend for less than $3.5 billion.

Monday March 20 market reaction:
- UBS stock dropped approximately 8% at open (integration risk concerns)
- CS stock traded near the offer price
- European banks: mixed, some relief that contagion was contained
- AT1 bonds globally: dropped 5-15%, repricing the asset class
