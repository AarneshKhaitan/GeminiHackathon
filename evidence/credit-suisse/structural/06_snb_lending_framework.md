# SNB Lending Framework

---

## Legal Basis

- **National Bank Act (Art. 5(2)(e))**: SNB shall "contribute to the stability of the financial system"
- **National Bank Act (Art. 9)**: SNB may "grant credit to banks and other financial market participants against adequate collateral"
- **Banking Act (Art. 30)**: In a restructuring, FINMA can request SNB to provide liquidity support

## Emergency Liquidity Assistance (ELA)

- SNB provides **collateralized** lending only — the borrower must pledge assets
- Eligible collateral includes: mortgage portfolios, government bonds, investment-grade corporate bonds
- The SNB does not provide unsecured lending to banks
- ELA is at the SNB's discretion — banks have no automatic right to it
- ELA carries a penalty rate above the market rate

## Comparison: Central Bank Emergency Lending

| Feature | ECB | Fed (Discount Window) | SNB |
|---------|-----|----------------------|-----|
| Collateral required | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Government guarantee available | Via national central banks | Yes (13(3) facilities) | Requires Federal Council action |

| Lender of last resort explicit | Yes | Yes | Implicit only |
