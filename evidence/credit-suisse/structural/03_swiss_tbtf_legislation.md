# Swiss "Too Big To Fail" (TBTF) Framework

Source: FINMA website, "The Swiss too-big-to-fail regime — Banks"

## Systemically Important Banks (from FINMA)

"Four institutions are designated as systemically important: UBS, the Raiffeisen Group, Zurcher Kantonalbank and PostFinance." These banks provide critical functions including domestic deposit and lending services plus payment systems that cannot be quickly replaced.

[Note: Credit Suisse was also designated as systemically important until its March 2023 acquisition by UBS. At the time of the crisis, it was one of only two Swiss G-SIBs alongside UBS.]

## Enhanced Capital and Liquidity Requirements (from FINMA)

"Systemically important banks must maintain elevated capital and liquidity buffers to strengthen their ability to withstand financial stress. These heightened standards make institutions more resilient during crises."

## Recovery Planning (from FINMA)

"Banks must annually demonstrate to FINMA how they can stabilize themselves during distress. Recovery plans must be effective at both liquidity and capital levels and adaptable across different scenarios. FINMA approves all recovery plans submitted."

## Emergency and Resolution Planning (from FINMA)

Each systemically important bank must show how its critical Swiss functions could "continue uninterrupted if they were at risk of insolvency." FINMA develops resolution plans outlining how it would restructure a failing systemically important bank. The regulator also conducts annual resolvability assessments, particularly for UBS.

## Resolution Tools Available

The TBTF framework provides authorities with:
1. **Bail-in**: Write down or convert liabilities to equity
2. **Transfer**: Transfer critical functions to a bridge bank or third party
3. **Separation**: Separate systemically important functions from the rest
4. **Liquidation**: Orderly wind-down of non-systemic parts

## Oversight (from FINMA)

"FINMA regularly reviews emergency plan measures for effectiveness and feasibility, and can impose specific corrective measures if needed. Public reporting occurs regularly on crisis planning status and resolvability assessments."

## What Actually Happened

The formal TBTF resolution framework was NOT used for Credit Suisse. Instead, the Federal Council enacted an Emergency Ordinance (March 16-19, 2023) operating outside the normal TBTF framework. The merger was arranged through ad hoc political negotiation over a single weekend, not the formal resolution process.
