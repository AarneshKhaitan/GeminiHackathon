# Credit Suisse AT1 Bond Prospectus — Point of Non-Viability (PONV) Clause

## AT1 Instruments Overview

Credit Suisse had 13 publicly issued AT1 instruments, issued between 2013 and 2022, denominated in USD, CHF, and SGD. Total outstanding: approximately CHF 16 billion (~$17.3B). Primarily held by institutional investors.

## The Two Write-Down Triggers (from prospectus terms)

### 1. Contingency Event (Automatic Trigger)
The CET1 ratio falls below **7 percent**. This is a mechanical, quantitative trigger — if CS's Common Equity Tier 1 ratio breaches 7%, the AT1 bonds automatically write down. No regulatory discretion needed.

### 2. Viability Event (Regulatory Trigger — the PONV)
A "Viability Event" was defined as occurring if:

**(a)** FINMA notifies Credit Suisse that it has determined that a write-down is "essential to prevent CS from becoming insolvent" because customary measures to improve the bank's capital adequacy are inadequate or unfeasible;

**OR**

**(b)** Credit Suisse receives an "irrevocable commitment of extraordinary support from the public sector" (i.e., a government bailout).

## Write-Down Mechanics

Credit Suisse's AT1 bonds specified **write-down to zero** (not conversion to equity). Upon a Viability Event or Contingency Event, the bonds would be written down "in whole" — full principal loss, no recovery.

## FINMA's Application (from FINMA press release, March 23, 2023)

FINMA explained the basis for the write-down:

- The AT1 instruments contained provisions for complete write-down upon a "Viability Event," specifically when "extraordinary government support is granted"
- The liquidity assistance loans secured by federal guarantee on March 19, 2023 triggered these contractual conditions
- The Federal Council's Emergency Ordinance authorized FINMA to order the write-down
- All 13 publicly issued AT1 instruments were written down to zero
- Tier 2 bonds were NOT affected
- Equity holders received CHF 0.76/share in UBS stock (~$3.25B total)

This was the largest AT1 write-down in history and the first time AT1 bondholders were wiped out while equity holders retained value.

## Subordination Clause

The AT1 bonds contained subordination clauses stating they ranked "senior to... all holders of Junior Capital" — meaning they should be senior to equity in the normal hierarchy. The write-down provisions created an exception: when a Viability Event is triggered, the write-down overrides normal priority.

## Subsequent Legal Challenge

The Swiss Federal Administrative Court (October 2025) found that FINMA's write-down order lacked solid legal basis — the CS prospectus clause (Clause 7(a)(iii)) was narrower than the statutory mechanism, and the specific conditions for triggering it were not properly met. The court found violations of constitutional requirements. FINMA appealed.
