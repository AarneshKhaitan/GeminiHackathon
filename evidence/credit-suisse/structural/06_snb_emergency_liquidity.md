# Swiss National Bank Emergency Liquidity Facility

## Source: Thomas Jordan Speech at BIS (November 2023)

SNB Chairman Thomas Jordan delivered a speech titled "The Swiss National Bank's role as lender of last resort in the crisis at Credit Suisse" providing the definitive account of the liquidity support.

### Key Quote

"Never before had a central bank provided such a large amount of liquidity to a single bank."

### Peak Liquidity Support

- **CHF 168 billion** across three currencies at maximum
- Made available "on the same days the liquidity was requested"

### Facility Types

1. **ELA (Emergency Liquidity Assistance)** — Classic emergency facility, collateralized, available under existing SNB framework
2. **ELA+ (Additional Liquidity Assistance)** — Created by Federal Council emergency ordinance (March 16, 2023), government-guaranteed
3. **PLB (Public Liquidity Backstop)** — Government-guaranteed facility, no collateral required — unprecedented measure

### Critical Assessment (Jordan quote)

"Without ELA+ Credit Suisse would have been in jeopardy of being unable to meet its financial obligations."

### Lessons Learned (from Jordan speech)

- Customer deposit outflows now occur faster and larger than regulations assumed
- Banks must prepare sufficient eligible collateral in advance
- Effective Public Liquidity Backstop needed for undercollateralized situations
- ELA+ should remain an emergency measure, not a permanent instrument
- "The liquidity regulations must be geared to the new reality of potentially faster and larger outflows"

## Timeline of Liquidity Support

### March 15-16, 2023 — Initial Emergency Draw

After CS stock crashed ~30% on March 15 (following the Saudi chairman interview):

- CS drew approximately **CHF 50 billion** (~$54B USD) on March 16 under existing CLF and ELA facilities, plus additional sources
- Source: CNBC reported "Credit Suisse to borrow up to about $54 billion from Swiss National Bank"

### March 19, 2023 — Expanded Facilities for Merger

When the UBS merger was announced, the Federal Council created ELA+ and PLB facilities. The SNB made available up to **CHF 200 billion** through combined facilities.

### Progression

The shift from collateralized lending (CLF/ELA) to government-guaranteed uncollateralized lending (PLB) in 72 hours signals each level of support proved insufficient.

## Autumn 2022 Context (from Jordan speech)

Jordan revealed that authorities had already been examining alternatives "including bank resolution or temporary public ownership" as early as autumn 2022 — months before the March crisis.
