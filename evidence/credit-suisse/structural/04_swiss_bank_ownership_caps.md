# Bank Ownership Regulatory Thresholds

---

## Swiss FINMA Thresholds

Under the Swiss Banking Act and FINMA Circular 2008/3:
- **3%**: Notification obligation to the exchange (SIX)
- **5%**: Enhanced disclosure requirements
- **10%**: Requires FINMA approval as a "qualified participation"
- **20%**: Triggers additional FINMA scrutiny and conditions
- **33%**: Effective control threshold under Swiss corporate law

A shareholder exceeding 10% in a Swiss bank must:
- Obtain prior FINMA authorization
- Meet "fit and proper" requirements
- Demonstrate adequate financial resources
- Accept ongoing supervisory obligations

## EU/EEA "Qualifying Holdings" (CRD IV, Art. 22-27)

- **10%**: Acquisition of a "qualifying holding" requires prior approval from the host country regulator
- **20%, 33%, 50%**: Each threshold crossing requires separate regulatory notification and approval
- Assessment period: Up to 60 working days for regulator to review

## Saudi Arabia (SAMA)

Saudi Arabian Monetary Authority (SAMA) imposes:
- Enhanced scrutiny for Saudi financial institutions taking stakes above 10% in foreign banks
- Cross-border investment limits for Saudi banks
- Board approval requirements for significant foreign exposures
