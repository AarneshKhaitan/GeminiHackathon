# Saudi National Bank Ownership Cap — Structural Constraint

## The Investment (October 2022)

Saudi National Bank (SNB) acquired a 9.9% stake in Credit Suisse for approximately CHF 1.4 billion ($1.5B), making it the bank's largest shareholder. The investment was part of CS's CHF 4 billion capital raise to fund its restructuring plan.

Source: CNBC (October 31, 2022) — At the time of purchase, SNB's chairman described CS shares as "a steal" at the entry price.

## The Regulatory Constraint — Why 9.9%

The 9.9% stake sat precisely at the maximum threshold below which multiple regulatory triggers are avoided. As reported across multiple outlets:

- If SNB went above 10%, additional regulatory obligations would be triggered from multiple jurisdictions
- Saudi banking regulations (SAMA) impose enhanced scrutiny for foreign financial institution stakes above 10%
- Swiss regulations trigger additional notifications and conditions above 10%
- EU/EEA "qualifying holding" provisions require regulatory approval above 10%

## Chairman Al Khudairy's Bloomberg TV Interview (March 15, 2023)

When asked if SNB would increase its stake in Credit Suisse, Chairman Ammar Al Khudairy stated (quoted in Bloomberg TV, widely reported):

**"Absolutely not, for many reasons beyond the simplest reason, which is regulatory and statutory."**

He elaborated: **"If we go above 10%, all new rules kick in whether it be by our regulator or the Swiss regulator or the European regulator."**

**"We're not inclined to get into a new regulatory regime. I can cite five or six other reasons, but one reason is there is a glass ceiling and we're not going to entertain going beyond it."**

## Market Impact

- CS stock fell approximately 30% intraday on March 15 after the interview
- The market interpreted "our biggest shareholder won't save us" — even though Al Khudairy was stating a structural regulatory fact

## Aftermath

- Al Khudairy resigned as SNB chairman on March 27, 2023, "due to personal reasons" (reported by CNN, CNBC, Al Jazeera)
- SNB's CHF 1.4B investment was reduced to approximately CHF 280M when UBS acquired CS at CHF 0.76/share
- Total loss for SNB: over $1 billion (reported by Fortune, March 20, 2023)
