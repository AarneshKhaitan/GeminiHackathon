# Credit Suisse Capital Structure (Q4 2022)

## Key Capital Ratios (as of December 31, 2022)

| Metric | Value | Regulatory Minimum |
|--------|-------|--------------------|
| CET1 Ratio | 14.1% | ~10% (FINMA requirement for G-SIBs) |
| Tier 1 Ratio | 20.0% | ~14.3% |
| Total Capital Ratio | ~25% | ~17% |
| CET1 Leverage Ratio | 4.3% | 3.5% |
| Tier 1 Leverage Ratio | 7.7% | 5.0% |

| Component | Approximate Amount |
|-----------|-------------------|
| CET1 (Common Equity Tier 1) | ~CHF 36B |

| Total Assets | ~CHF 530B |

## AT1 as Percentage of Capital

AT1 instruments represented approximately **25%** of Credit Suisse's total regulatory capital. This is significant because:
- If AT1 is written down, a quarter of the capital buffer disappears instantly
- But the bank can still meet minimum CET1 requirements (14.1% vs 10% minimum)
- This is why equity survived while AT1 didn't — there was enough CET1 to keep the bank above minimum requirements even without AT1

## The Paradox: Strong Capital Ratios, Dying Bank

The Q4 2022 numbers show CS comfortably above all regulatory minimums. However, adequate capital ratios did not mean the bank was safe:

**Why "adequate capital" doesn't mean "safe":**

1. **Capital ratios are backward-looking** — they reflect the balance sheet as of Dec 31, not the real-time situation in March
2. **Deposit flight was accelerating** — Q4 2022 disclosed CHF 110B in deposit outflows during Q4 alone
3. **Capital doesn't equal liquidity** — you can have strong capital ratios and still run out of cash if depositors flee faster than you can liquidate assets
4. **Mark-to-market losses aren't reflected** — securities portfolios may have unrealized losses not captured in regulatory ratios

## The Q4 2022 Earnings Release (February 9, 2023)

Key disclosures from the earnings call:
- **Net loss:** CHF 7.3 billion for full year 2022
- **Q4 net loss:** CHF 1.4 billion
- **Deposit outflows:** CHF 110.5 billion in Q4 2022 (October-December)
  - This is the critical number — not the capital ratios
  - CHF 110B in deposit flight in a single quarter is catastrophic
  - Even though deposits stabilized somewhat in January 2023, the damage to confidence was done
- **Revenue decline:** Investment banking revenue down significantly
- **Restructuring costs:** Ongoing charges from the strategic transformation

## What the Numbers Hide

The capital structure was adequate for normal times but masked several risks:

1. **Liquidity vs. Solvency**: CET1 of 14.1% suggests solvency. But CHF 110B in deposit outflows suggests a liquidity crisis. Banks fail from liquidity crises faster than solvency crises.

2. **AT1 Dependence**: With AT1 making up 25% of total capital, the bank's capital adequacy was partially dependent on instruments that could be wiped out in a crisis — the very crisis that the capital was supposed to protect against.

3. **Risk-Weighted Asset Sensitivity**: The 14.1% CET1 ratio depends on the denominator (RWA calculations). Under stress, RWAs can increase as asset quality deteriorates, causing the ratio to drop even without capital losses.
